Testosterone cypionate is the predominant injectable testosterone formulation prescribed in the United States for hormone replacement therapy. Structurally nearly identical to testosterone enanthate, the cypionate ester differs only slightly in carbon chain length, resulting in a marginally longer half-life. It remains the standard of care for hypogonadism and is extensively studied in clinical literature.

With a half-life of approximately 8 days, testosterone cypionate supports once-weekly or twice-weekly injection protocols. Serum testosterone levels rise within 24–48 hours after administration and maintain elevated concentrations for roughly 7–10 days before declining. The default dose of 250 mg per week is a common starting point for both clinical and performance applications, though individual response varies considerably.

Like other testosterone esters, cypionate undergoes aromatization and 5-alpha reduction, producing estradiol and dihydrotestosterone respectively. Monitoring complete blood counts, lipid panels, and hormone levels is recommended during use. Subcutaneous and intramuscular routes are both viable, with subcutaneous administration gaining popularity for its convenience and comparable pharmacokinetics.
