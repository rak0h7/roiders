Stanozolol, widely recognized as Winstrol, is an oral and injectable DHT-derived anabolic steroid that has been used clinically and in athletics for decades. The 17-alpha-alkylated oral formulation bypasses first-pass hepatic metabolism to remain orally bioavailable, but this modification also imparts hepatotoxicity risk. Stanozolol does not aromatize and is favored for cutting cycles where dry, vascular physiques are the goal.

The oral form has a half-life of approximately 8 hours (0.33 days), requiring twice-daily dosing to maintain stable blood levels. A default dose of 25 mg per day is moderate, with effective ranges typically between 25–50 mg daily. Stanozolol lowers sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG) more dramatically than most other steroids, effectively increasing free testosterone and the activity of co-administered compounds.

Hepatotoxicity is a primary concern with oral stanozolol, and liver function monitoring is essential during use. The compound is also notorious for causing joint dryness and discomfort by reducing synovial fluid, making it poorly suited for athletes in sports requiring joint resilience. Injectable stanozolol (water-based) is also available but shares the same pharmacological profile. Detection time for the oral form is approximately 3 weeks.
