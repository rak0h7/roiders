Nandrolone phenylpropionate (NPP) is the shorter-acting counterpart to nandrolone decanoate, offering the same 19-nor anabolic profile with a more manageable pharmacokinetic window. The phenylpropionate ester requires injections every 3–4 days to maintain stable blood levels, but allows users to titrate or discontinue the compound more rapidly than the decanoate version. NPP has seen a resurgence in popularity as access to pharmaceutical-grade nandrolone has shifted.

With a half-life of approximately 4 days, NPP reaches peak serum levels within 24–48 hours and requires more frequent administration than Deca. A default dose of 100 mg every 3 days (roughly 230 mg per week) is typical, though many users run 300–400 mg weekly for anabolic effects. The shorter ester is often perceived to produce less water retention and bloating compared to the longer decanoate ester.

As with all 19-nor compounds, NPP suppresses endogenous testosterone production and carries progestogenic activity. Stacking with a testosterone base is considered essential to maintain libido and overall well-being. Detection times, while shorter than decanoate, still extend to approximately 12 months due to the persistent nandrolone metabolites that remain in fatty tissue and are slowly released over time.
