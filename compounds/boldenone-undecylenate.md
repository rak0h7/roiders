Boldenone undecylenate, originally marketed as Equipoise for veterinary applications, is a long-acting anabolic steroid derived from testosterone with a double bond at the C1–C2 position. This structural modification reduces its androgenic potency and aromatization rate compared to testosterone, while maintaining strong anabolic activity. It is valued for producing steady, lean gains with enhanced appetite and erythropoiesis stimulation.

The undecylenate ester provides one of the longest half-lives among common injectable compounds at approximately 14 days. Weekly injections of 300–600 mg are typical, with 300 mg serving as a moderate starting dose. Boldenone raises red blood cell production significantly, which can improve endurance but also increases hematocrit to levels requiring monitoring and potential therapeutic phlebotomy.

Boldenone aromatizes at roughly half the rate of testosterone, making estrogenic side effects less common but still possible at higher doses. A characteristic side effect is increased vascularity and a "polished" physique appearance due to its effects on nitrogen retention and collagen synthesis. The compound has a relatively long detection window of approximately 5 months, and its slow clearance means planning adequate time off before tested competition is essential.
