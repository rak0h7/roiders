Nandrolone decanoate, widely known by its former brand name Deca-Durabolin, is a long-acting 19-nortestosterone derivative prized for its anabolic properties and relatively mild androgenic profile. As a 19-nor compound, it lacks the C-19 methyl group found in testosterone, which alters its receptor binding characteristics and metabolic pathway. It is frequently stacked with testosterone to maintain physiological androgen levels and mitigate sexual side effects.

The decanoate ester provides a half-life of approximately 15 days, allowing for once-weekly injections. Typical dosing ranges from 200–400 mg per week, with 200 mg being a conservative starting point. Nandrolone exhibits significant progestogenic activity and aromatizes at roughly 20% the rate of testosterone, though its metabolites can still contribute to estrogenic effects through alternative pathways.

Notable side effects include suppression of endogenous testosterone, potential prolactin elevation, and the infamous "Deca Dick"—a form of erectile dysfunction attributed to progestogenic and neurochemical effects. Nandrolone metabolites are detectable for up to 18 months, making it one of the longest-detectable anabolic compounds. Joint lubrication and collagen synthesis benefits are commonly reported, contributing to its popularity in therapeutic and athletic contexts.
