Oxandrolone, marketed as Anavar, is a mild oral DHT-derived anabolic steroid with an established clinical history for treating muscle wasting conditions, burns, and osteoporosis. Its favorable anabolic-to-androgenic ratio and relatively low hepatotoxicity compared to other 17-alpha-alkylated orals have made it one of the most popular compounds among both male and female users. Oxandrolone does not aromatize and does not convert to DHT, as it is already DHT-derived.

With a half-life of approximately 9 hours (0.375 days), oxandrolone is typically dosed twice daily. A default dose of 25 mg per day is a starting point for male users, with effective ranges from 20–80 mg daily depending on goals and experience. Female users often employ 5–20 mg daily due to the risk of virilization at higher doses. The compound is valued for producing lean gains with minimal water retention.

Despite its reputation as a mild steroid, oxandrolone still suppresses endogenous testosterone and can impact lipid profiles by reducing HDL cholesterol. The 17-alpha-alkylated structure carries hepatotoxic potential, though at lower doses and reasonable cycle lengths, liver stress is generally manageable. Oxandrolone is among the most counterfeited oral steroids, making source verification particularly important. Detection time is approximately 3 weeks.
