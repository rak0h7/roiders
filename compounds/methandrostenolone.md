Methandrostenolone, universally known as Dianabol or Dbol, is one of the oldest and most iconic oral anabolic steroids. Developed in the 1950s, it is a testosterone derivative with modifications at the C-17 alpha position (for oral bioavailability) and C-1–C2 double bond (to reduce androgenicity). Dianabol is primarily used as a bulking agent, producing rapid increases in strength and body mass, largely attributed to glycogen retention and intracellular water.

The half-life of approximately 6 hours (0.25 days) necessitates split daily dosing—typically 2–3 times per day—to maintain stable blood levels. A default dose of 30 mg per day is moderate, with common protocols ranging from 20–50 mg daily. Users often report dramatic strength increases within the first week, along with noticeable bloating and water retention due to aromatization and estrogenic activity.

Hepatotoxicity is a significant concern given the 17-alpha-alkylated structure, and cycle lengths are generally limited to 4–6 weeks. Methandrostenolone aromatizes readily, making estrogen management important for controlling gynecomastia and excessive water retention. Despite its oral-only format, it suppresses endogenous testosterone production substantially. Detection time extends to approximately 6 weeks, longer than many other orals due to its metabolite profile.
