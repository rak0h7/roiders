Testosterone enanthate is one of the most widely used injectable testosterone esters in clinical and performance contexts. The enanthate ester extends the release of testosterone from the injection site, providing a relatively stable serum concentration when administered on a weekly or bi-weekly schedule. It serves as a foundational compound for hormone replacement therapy and is commonly used as the backbone of many anabolic cycles.

The pharmacokinetic profile of testosterone enanthate features a half-life of approximately 7 days, meaning peak levels occur within 24–48 hours post-injection and gradually decline over the following week. Typical therapeutic dosing ranges from 100–250 mg per week for TRT, while performance-oriented protocols often employ 250–500 mg weekly, sometimes split into two injections for smoother blood levels.

As a base compound, testosterone enanthate aromatizes to estradiol via the aromatase enzyme, which can lead to estrogen-related side effects at higher doses. Users often monitor estradiol levels and may incorporate an aromatase inhibitor if needed. Injection site reactions, elevated hematocrit, and suppression of endogenous testosterone production are among the primary considerations when using this compound.
