Anastrozole is a non-steroidal aromatase inhibitor (AI) that selectively blocks the conversion of androgens to estrogens by binding to the aromatase enzyme. Originally developed for the treatment of hormone receptor-positive breast cancer, it has become a cornerstone ancillary in testosterone replacement therapy and anabolic steroid cycles where estrogen management is required. Unlike SERMs, anastrozole reduces total circulating estrogen rather than selectively blocking receptor sites.

With a half-life of approximately 2 days, anastrozole maintains effective aromatase inhibition with dosing every other day or twice weekly. A default dose of 0.5 mg is standard, though TRT patients may require as little as 0.25 mg twice weekly while those on high-dose testosterone cycles may need 0.5 mg every other day. Blood work monitoring of estradiol levels is critical, as over-suppression of estrogen can cause joint pain, lipid deterioration, mood disturbances, and impaired libido.

Anastrozole is taken orally and reaches peak plasma concentration within 2 hours of administration. Common side effects include headache, hot flashes, and fatigue, particularly when estrogen is driven below physiological ranges. It does not affect progestogenic or androgenic pathways directly. Detection time in athletic testing is approximately 2 weeks, and the compound is prohibited in tested sports when used to mask the estrogenic effects of anabolic agents.
